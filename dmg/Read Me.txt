clip and cue — Read Me
==================================================

Thanks for downloading clip and cue — a tiny menu bar app that
remembers your last 9 clipboard copies so you can paste any of
them back.


INSTALL
--------------------------------------------------
Drag the clip and cue icon onto the Applications folder
(follow the arrow above).


FIRST LAUNCH — PLEASE READ
--------------------------------------------------
clip and cue isn't notarized by Apple yet, so the first time you
open it macOS may say:

    "clipandcue.app Not Opened —
     Apple could not verify it is free of malware."

This is expected for a small independent app. Here's how to open it:

macOS Sequoia (15) and later:
    1. Double-click clip and cue, then click "Done" on the warning.
    2. Open  System Settings  →  Privacy & Security.
    3. Scroll down to the "Security" section. You'll see:
         "clipandcue.app was blocked to protect your Mac."
    4. Click "Open Anyway", then confirm and authenticate
       (Touch ID or your password).
    clip and cue opens, and won't ask again.

macOS Sonoma (14) and earlier:
    Right-click (Control-click) clip and cue in Applications,
    choose "Open", then click "Open" in the dialog.


PERMISSIONS YOU MAY BE ASKED FOR
--------------------------------------------------
  • Accessibility — so it can paste into other apps (one-time prompt).
  • Notes — only if you use "New note" from the menu.


USING IT
--------------------------------------------------
  • Click the menu bar icon to see your recent copies; click one to paste.
  • Or press  ⌘⌥V  and tap  1–9.
